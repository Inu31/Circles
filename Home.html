Introduction

You may have come across objects in daily life, which are round in shape such as wheels of a vehcile, bangles, dials of many clocks, coins, key rings, button of shirts, etc. In a clock, you might have observed that the second's hand goes rund the dial of the clock rapidly and its top moves in a round path. This path traced by the tip of the second's hand is called a circle.

What is a circle?

Take a compass and fix a pencil in it. Put its pointed leg on a point on a sheet of a paper. Open the other leg to some distance. Keeping the poined leg on the same point, rotate the other leg through one revolution. The figure formed is called a circle. The fixed point is called the center of the circle and the fixed distance is called the radius of the circle.
